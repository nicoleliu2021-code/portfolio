# CRO Case Study: Intent-Aligned Messaging → +52% Application Lift

---

**Funnel:** Landing page | **Traffic:** Paid / affiliate (rate-shopping) | **Goal:** Increase application starts

---

## Problem
Generic, guidance-oriented messaging failed to match the specific intent of rate-shopping users from paid channels. Mismatch at first impression created friction and bounce.

## Hypothesis
Shift headline and messaging to align with user's rate/savings intent, reducing bounce and increasing commitment through intent-message match.

## Experiment
Tested savings-focused, rate-comparison headline vs generic control on paid and affiliate traffic.

**Control:** "Find the right mortgage for your needs"
**Variant:** Direct, savings-focused headline anchored to rate comparison

---

## Results

| Metric | Lift |
|--------|------|
| Visit-to-Lead | **+7.19%** |
| Visit-to-Credit Pull | **+19.2%** |
| Visit-to-Application | **+52.3%** |

---

## Key Learning
**Specific, intent-matched messaging outperforms broad language for comparison-driven audiences.**

Users from paid channels are shopping, not browsing. By reframing the experience to speak directly to that intent, we reduced bounce, increased trust, and lowered friction at the application stage. Alignment at the top creates momentum throughout.

---

**Takeaway:** For paid traffic, intent alignment at first impression is not optional. Generic messaging is a conversion tax. Specific, action-oriented framing drives high-value actions.
