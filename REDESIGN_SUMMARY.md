# Portfolio Redesign: AI-First PM Positioning

## 🎯 Core Strategy

**From:** Generic "Product Manager who builds"  
**To:** **"AI-First Product Manager who ships, experiments, and builds from scratch"**

---

## 📊 What Changed & Why

### 1. **Hero Section** → Complete Rewrite

**Before:**
- Generic badge: "Product Manager × Builder × Strategist"
- Centered layout with standard stats grid
- Passive language: "I design and launch..."
- AI mentioned but not emphasized

**After:**
- **AI-First Badge:** "AI-First Product Manager" (immediate differentiation)
- **Stronger Headline:** "I ship AI products, run rigorous experiments, and build from scratch"
- **Contrast-based value prop:** "Most PMs write docs. I write code. Most PMs theorize. I run experiments..."
- **Left-aligned, editorial layout:** More premium, less centered-template feel
- **Cleaner stats:** Inline with context instead of grid

**Why:** Recruiters decide in 3 seconds. New hero immediately signals differentiation.

---

### 2. **New "How I Think" Section** → Added to Homepage

**What:** Prominent section showcasing 4 core principles with icons:
1. "AI isn't a feature, it's a platform shift"
2. "Ship to learn, don't learn to ship"
3. "PMs who code have unfair advantages"
4. "Growth compounds, features don't"

**Why:** 
- Shows strategic thinking upfront (not buried in About page)
- Differentiates from standard PM portfolios
- Gives recruiters quick understanding of your approach
- Showcases AI-first mindset prominently

---

### 3. **About Page** → Redesigned as "My Approach"

**Before:**
- Resume-style timeline
- Generic "My Story" narrative
- Philosophy buried at bottom
- List of industries/skills

**After:**
- **Philosophy-first structure**
- **4 deep-dive principles:**
  - AI-first product thinking (with specific examples)
  - Experimentation over opinions (with $4.2M proof point)
  - Technical depth as leverage (why you code)
  - Ship to learn, iterate to win (with 4-week MVP example)
- **"What I look for in roles"** section (shows intentionality)
- **Condensed career highlights** (3 roles, not full timeline)
- **Strong CTA:** "Let's build something"

**Why:** 
- Less resume, more strategic POV
- Shows how you think, not just what you've done
- More recruiter-friendly (scanable principles)
- Positions you as thoughtful, not just experienced

---

### 4. **Navigation** → Streamlined

**Before:**
- 9 navigation items (Home, Work, Projects, Experiments, Teardowns, Thinking, Build Log, About, Resume)
- Cluttered and dilutes focus

**After:**
- **4 key items:** Work | Lab | Approach | Resume
- Logo with icon for visual interest
- "Lab" consolidates Projects/Experiments/Teardowns
- "Approach" renamed from "About"

**Why:**
- Better scanability
- Focuses recruiter attention on what matters
- More premium/refined feel
- Clearer information architecture

---

### 5. **Featured Work Section** → Improved Copy

**Before:**
- "Real product work across fintech, growth, and builder projects"

**After:**
- "Product launches, growth experiments, and side projects. Each with measurable business impact."
- Added "Live project" indicators for shipped products
- Better section title: "Selected work" (more confident)

**Why:**
- More confident positioning
- Emphasizes measurable impact
- Shows shipping real products

---

## 🎨 Design Changes

### Visual Improvements:
1. **Cleaner hierarchy** - Less centered layouts, more editorial
2. **Better white space** - Removed unnecessary grid stats
3. **Subtle backgrounds** - Gray-50 for sections, white for content
4. **Icon consistency** - Using Lucide icons throughout
5. **Premium feel** - Less gradient overload, more refined

### Typography:
- Maintained bold headlines
- Added better line-height for readability
- Used gray-500 for de-emphasized text
- Consistent font weights

---

## ✅ Key Wins

### For Recruiters:
1. **3-second differentiation:** "AI-First PM" badge + headline
2. **Quick scanability:** "How I Think" cards
