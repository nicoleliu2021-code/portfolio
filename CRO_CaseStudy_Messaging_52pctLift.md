# CRO Case Study: Intent-Aligned Messaging Driving +52% Application Lift

---

## Snapshot

**Funnel Stage:** Landing page (first impression)
**Traffic Type:** High-intent affiliate / paid traffic (rate-shopping mindset)
**Primary Goal:** Increase application starts by aligning messaging with user search intent

---

## Problem

The existing landing page used generic, guidance-oriented messaging that failed to match the specific intent of users arriving from paid and affiliate channels.

These users were actively comparing rates and searching for savings opportunities, but the headline positioned the experience as educational rather than transactional. This mismatch created friction at the critical first impression.

---

## Hypothesis

**If** we shift the headline and supporting messaging to align more directly with the user's rate/savings intent,
**Then** more users will progress deeper into the funnel
**Because** the experience will better match why they clicked in the first place, reducing bounce and increasing commitment.

---

## Experiment Design

Tested a savings-focused, rate-comparison headline against the generic control.

- **Control:** Broad, guidance-oriented headline ("Find the right mortgage for your needs")
- **Variant:** Direct, savings-focused headline anchored to rate comparison and financial outcomes
- **Traffic Source:** Paid search and affiliate traffic (rate-shopping audiences)
- **Test Type:** A/B split test with equal traffic allocation
- **Primary Metrics:** Visit-to-lead, visit-to-credit pull, visit-to-application

---

## Results

| Metric | Lift |
|--------|------|
| **Visit-to-Lead** | **+7.19%** |
| **Visit-to-Credit Pull** | **+19.2%** |
| **Visit-to-Application** | **+52.3%** |

The variant showed progressively stronger lift as users moved deeper into the funnel, with the most dramatic impact on application starts — a clear signal that intent alignment reduced hesitation at the commitment stage.

---

## Key Learning

**Specific, intent-matched messaging outperforms broad mortgage language, especially for comparison-driven audiences.**

Users arriving from paid and affiliate channels are not browsing — they're shopping. They have a specific question ("Can I get a better rate?") and expect an answer, not education.

By reframing the experience to speak directly to that intent, we:
- Reduced bounce by setting the right expectation immediately
- Increased trust by demonstrating relevance (not generic advice)
- Lowered friction at the application stage by priming users for a transaction, not exploration

The compounding lift from lead → credit → application shows that alignment at the top of the funnel creates momentum throughout.

---

## Why It Matters

This experiment reinforces a core principle of high-intent CRO: **match the message to the mindset.**

Many landing pages are designed to appeal to a broad audience, but in doing so, they fail to speak to anyone specifically. For paid traffic — where every click has a cost and users have clear intent — generic messaging is a conversion tax.

Small shifts in headline framing can unlock major lifts in high-value actions, especially when those shifts reduce the gap between what the user expected and what they see.

---

**Operator Takeaway:**
For paid and affiliate traffic, intent alignment at first impression is not optional. Broad messaging may feel safer, but specific, action-oriented framing drives conversions.
